This module provides a comprehensive landing page for students to master Tree-based data structures through theory, interactive simulation, and self-assessment.

---

## 1. Theory & Concepts
Explore the fundamental principles of hierarchical data structures, their properties, and asymptotic analysis.

* **Tree Fundamentals**: Introduction to nodes, edges, height, and depth.
* **Binary Search Trees**: Understanding the property where $L < Root < R$ and its impact on $O(\log n)$ search time.
* **Self-Balancing Logic**: Exploring the necessity of AVL rotations (LL, RR, LR, RL) to maintain $O(\log n)$ worst-case performance.
* **Expression Trees**: Converting infix expressions to postfix and prefix using tree traversals.

---

## 3. Knowledge Check & Quizzes
Test your understanding of the concepts covered in this module.

1.  **Complexity Quiz**: What is the maximum height of an AVL tree with $n$ nodes?
2.  **Traversal Challenge**: Given an In-order and Pre-order sequence, can you uniquely reconstruct the original BST?
3.  **Rotation Logic**: Identify the required rotation type for a Right-Heavy subtree with a Left-Heavy child.

> **Note to Students:** These visualizers are designed to supplement your lab sessions. Please ensure you have reviewed the primary lecture notes before attempting the quizzes.
